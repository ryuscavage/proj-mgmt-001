The Big Picture: Hierarchy of Work

Projects don't exist in a vacuum. They are often part of larger programs and portfolios that align with the organization's strategic goals.

Portfolio Management

Aligns with strategic business goals.

Program Management

Coordinates a group of related projects.

Project Management

Delivers a unique, temporary endeavor.

Projects vs. Operations

It's crucial to distinguish between temporary projects that create change and ongoing operations that sustain the business.

Projects (Temporary)

  • Produces a unique product or service.
  • Has a definite beginning and end.
  • Focuses on achieving a specific objective.

Operations (Ongoing)

  • Produces repetitive outputs.
  • Is continuous and sustains the business.
  • Focuses on maintaining stability and efficiency.

The Human Element

Leadership vs. Management

While often intertwined, leadership and management represent different skill sets. Effective project managers balance both to guide their teams to success.

The PM Superpower

Emotional Intelligence (EQ)

EQ is the ability to understand and manage emotions in yourself and others. It is a critical factor in communication, motivation, and stakeholder relations.
